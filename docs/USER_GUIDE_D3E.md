# 📖 User Guide: Decision Intelligence Engine (D3E)

This guide explains how to use the **Data-Driven Decision Engine** to analyze geospatial data and make strategic operational decisions.

---

## 1. Accessing the Intelligence View
To open the engine:
1.  Locate the **Sidebar** on the left side of the dashboard.
2.  Click on the **Intelligence** icon (Shield/Brain icon).
3.  The interface will switch to a **Split-View**:
    *   **Left**: Tactical Map Analysis.
    *   **Right**: Decision Intelligence Panel.

---

## 2. Real-Time Viewport Analysis
The D3E works based on **"What you see is what you analyze."**
*   **Panning**: When you drag the map, the engine automatically re-calculates the scores for the new area.
*   **Zooming**: Zooming in provides a "Hyper-Local" score, while zooming out gives a "Regional Overview."
*   **Status Indicator**: Look for the pulsating green "Live Engine Active" light at the top of the map to ensure data is fetching.

---

## 3. Understanding the Intelligence Panel

### A. Suitability Score (The Percentage)
*   **0-40% (Sub-Optimal)**: The area does not meet the primary criteria. High risk or low infrastructure.
*   **40-70% (Favorable)**: Meets basic requirements but has specific bottlenecks (e.g., high crime or poor connectivity).
*   **70-100% (Optimal)**: A "Hot Zone." Highly recommended for deployment or investment.

### B. The Radar Chart
The Radar Chart provides a 360-degree view of **why** a score is high or low:
*   **Safety**: Derived from crime rates and incident volatility.
*   **Growth**: Derived from development index and infrastructure.
*   **Density**: Derived from population concentration.
*   **Transit**: Derived from connectivity and resource proximity.

### C. Tactical Observations
These are automated, bulleted insights generated by the engine. 
*   *Example:* "Development index is in the top 15th percentile."
*   *Example:* "High volatility recorded in recent crime trends."

### D. Strategic Recommendation
This is the **"Final Verdict."** It translates complex math into a human-readable instruction for decision-makers.

---

## 4. How to Make a Decision (The Workflow)
1.  **Define Goal**: Pan the map to your area of interest.
2.  **Identify Suitability**: Check the main Gauge. If it's green (>75%), proceed to evaluation.
3.  **Check the Balance**: Look at the Radar Chart. If you are doing a "Security Mission" but the Safety metric is very low, this confirms the need for more units.
4.  **Confirm via Recommendation**: Read the Strategic Card.
5.  **Execute**: Use the **Connect** tab (Chat/Video) to coordinate with teams in that sector.

---

## 5. Technical Note
The prototype currently uses a **Heuristic Model** based on geographic coordinates. In the next phase, this will be linked to live data from `data.gov.in`.
